References and Citations Guide

This document provides comprehensive APA-style citations and references for all sources used throughout the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Book, following APA 7th edition guidelines.

APA Citation Style Guidelines​

Basic Format for Different Source Types​

1. Books​

Single Author: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle. Publisher.

Example: Quigley, M., Gerkey, B., & Smart, W. (2022). Programming robots with ROS: A practical introduction to the robot operating system. O'Reilly Media.

2. Journal Articles​

Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Periodical, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxx/yyyy

Example: Schoellig, A. P., & Beard, R. W. (2021). Adaptive control of quadrotor aerial vehicles: A survey. IEEE Control Systems Magazine, 41(2), 30-54. https://doi.org/10.1109/MCS.2021.3052434

3. Conference Proceedings​

Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year, Month). Title of paper. In Proceedings of Conference Name (pp. page range). Publisher. URL or DOI

Example: Koenig, N., & Howard, A. (2004, September). Design and use paradigms for Gazebo, an open-source multi-robot simulator. In Proceedings of the 2004 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (pp. 2149-2154). IEEE. https://doi.org/10.1109/IROS.2004.1389727

4. Technical Reports and Documentation​

Organization Name. (Year). Title of report. URL

Example: Open Robotics. (2023). ROS 2 documentation: Humble Hawksbill. https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/

5. Software and Code​

Author, A. A. (Year). Software title [Computer software]. Publisher/Distribution platform. URL

Example: Lütkebohle, I. (2023). NVIDIA Isaac Sim [Robotics simulation software]. NVIDIA. https://developer.nvidia.com/isaac-sim

In-Text Citation Examples​

Parenthetical Citations​

  • Single author: (Smith, 2023)
  • Two authors: (Smith & Jones, 2023)
  • Three or more authors: (Smith et al., 2023)
  • Direct quote: (Smith, 2023, p. 45)

Narrative Citations​

  • Single author: Smith (2023) noted that...
  • Two authors: Smith and Jones (2023) found that...
  • Three or more authors: Smith et al. (2023) suggested that...

Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) and URLs​

DOI Format​

  • Include DOI when available: https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxxx
  • Use the DOI format for permanent access
  • Verify DOIs are active and accessible

URL Format​

  • Include retrieval date for sources likely to change: Retrieved October 15, 2023, from URL
  • Use stable URLs when possible
  • Verify URLs are accessible
